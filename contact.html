PNG Unitech Milne Bay Province Students & Staffs Association

Constitution

1.0 NAME:
The formal name of this organization is PNG UNITECH Milne Bay Students and Staffs Association (PNG UNITECH MBSSA) hereinafter referred to as the “Association”

2.0 Terms and Abbreviations
• PNG UNITECH MBSSA refers to the PNG Unitech Milne Bay Students and Staffs Association
• Executives refers to the elected executives of PNG UNITECH MBSSA

3.0 OBJECTIVES
I. To support and guide students achieve their prime purpose of studies in the University.
II. To organise and develop students participating in various non-academic activities.
III. To motivate and encourage students to be agents of change for the province and country.
IV. To provide a legal platform to lease between the PNG Unitech MBSSA and Milne Bay Provincial/District government.

4.0 DEDICATION OF PNG UNITECH MBSSA TO GOD
5.1 The Association is dedicated to Christianity and whole heartedly to God Almighty.

5.0 ATTAINING OBJECTS
The Association shall be empowered to do all things necessary which are incidental to and necessary for the attainment of the objects of the Association.

6.0 PROPERTY OF THE ASSOCIATION
The Association must apply all property and income of the association towards the promotion of the purposes of the association. No part of that property or income shall be paid or otherwise distributed, directly or indirectly, to members of the Association, except in good faith in the promotion of the intended purposes.

7.0 SPORTS
7.1 The Association shall register as many sporting codes as deemed necessary and feasible based upon the members’ interest, Participation and the University sporting section.
7.2 Sporting equipment shall be attained by the association within its capacity for the respective registered sporting code.
7.3 All sporting equipment purchased or donated to the association shall remain the sole property of the association.

8.0 POWERS OF THE ASSOCIATION
8.1 To acquire, hold, deal with, and dispose of any real or personal property;
8.2 To open and operate bank accounts;
8.3 To invest its money –
(i) in any security in which trust moneys may be invested; or
(ii) in any other manner authorised by the rules of the Association;

8.4 To borrow money upon such terms and conditions as the Association thinks fir;
8.5 To give such security for the discharge of liabilities incurred by the Association as the management committee deems fits;
8.6 To appoint agents and representatives to transact any business of the Association on its behalf for reward or otherwise;
8.7 To build, construct, erect, maintain, alter and repair any premises building or other structure of any kind and to furnish equip and improve the same for use by the Association;
8.8 Accept donations and gifts in accordance with the purpose of the Association;
8.9 Print and publish any information by any media including newsletters, newspapers, articles or leaflets for promotion of the Association;
8.10 Provide gifts and prizes and certificates in accordance with the objects of the Association;
8.11 Organise social events for Members and the promotion of the Association; and
8.12 To enter into any other contract the Association considers necessary or desirable.

9.0 MEMBERSHIP
9.1 Membership shall be open to any person who wishes to further the interests of the Association. This is only applicable to Milne Bay Students or students who are mixed parentage of Milne Bay.
9.2 Any person seeking membership shall make application to the Management Committee, and the Management Committee shall determine whether the application is successful or not.
9.3 Each person admitted to membership shall be;
9.3.1 Bound by the Constitution and By-laws of the Association.
9.3.2 Come liable for such fees and subscriptions as may be fixed by the Association.
9.3.3 Entitled to all advantages and privileges of membership.
9.4 Membership Categories:

9.4.1 ORDINARY MEMBER

Any person who is a financial member of the Association is entitled to hold any office and enjoy the privileges of the Association.

9.4.2 SOCIAL MEMBER
Persons other than ordinary members who are interested in promoting the Association, but who do not wish to participate in the playing activities of the Association, may become a Social Member.

9.4.3 PATRON
The Association may, at its discretion, elect a patron/s or vice patron/s of the Association for such period as may be deemed necessary. Such patron/s or vice patron/s shall not be eligible to vote unless they are current members of the Association under another category of membership. Refer to Section 13.0

9.4.4 AFFILIATED CLUB’S OR TEAMS
A club or team desirous of becoming an affiliated club or team must take application in accordance with the by-laws of the Association. Such application must be lodged with the Association Secretary on or before a date as determined by the Management Committee of the Association. Each affiliated club or team shall appoint or elect a delegate as his or her representative to meetings of the Management Committee.
9.5 The Management Committee shall appoint a member of the Management Committee to maintain an up-to-date register of members of the Association.
9.6 A member may at any reasonable time inspect the records and documents of the Association.

10.0 SOCIAL NIGHT
10.1 The association shall host social nights on a scheduled basis in line of the objectives of the association
10.2 Female members of the association shall take the lead to organise with the assistance of the executives and the members.
10.3 The association shall provide snacks and refreshments (tea/coffee/sugar) to host the night .
10.4 Members shall be open to meet and greet new members in first years to final years .

11.0 SUBSCRIPTIONS
11.1 All Milne Bay students from first to final year shall pay a non-refundable subscription or membership fee of k10 after fully being registered into the University.

12.0 TERMINATION OF MEMBERSHIP
12.1 Any person’s membership may be terminated by the following events;
12.1.1 Registration
12.1.2 Expulsion
12.1.3 A member’s annual membership fee remains unpaid after mid semester break of the second semester of the PNG University of Technology’s academic year falling due;
12.2 The Management Committee shall have the power to suspend or expel any member of the association for;
12.2.1 any of the events in item 11.1
12.2.2 false or inaccurate statements made in the member’s application for membership of the association,
12.2.3 breach of any rule, regulation or by law of the association and
12.2.4 by any act detrimental to the association. After having undertaken due inquiry.
12.3 Any member who is expelled, suspended or has the membership terminated, shall have the right to appeal against their suspension or expulsion by presenting their case to the General Meeting called for such purpose, and the decision of the General Meeting shall be final.

13.0 EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE & DUTIES/ RESPONSIBILITIES
13.1 Management of the Association shall be vested in the Management Committee elected by the members of the Annual General Meeting and consisting of;
13.1.1 President: This person serves as the primary student contact for the organisation; the “external spokesperson” of the group that regularly interact with other student organisations and university officials.
13.1.2 Vice President: This person serves the same role as the president.
13.1.3 Secretary: This person/s will be responsible for the documentation and record keeping of the association matters.
13.1.4 Treasurer: This person/s will be primarily responsible for the finances of the association.
13.1.5 Events and Fundraising Coordinator: This person/s will be responsible for doing up schedules, and plan events for any fundraising activities and other organised activity to be performed by the association. Fundraising Coordinator helps specifically in fundraising plans/tasks while adhering to the Events Coordinators instructions.
13.1.6 Chairman: This person/s ensures that meetings are planned effectively, conducted according to the constitution and that association matters are dealt with in an efficient manner.
13.1.7 Media Representative: This person/s will be responsible for any and all media related activity for the association.
13.2 The candidates for the president seat are open to all students but is suitable for senior experienced students .
13.3 The other 5 executive committees shall be open to the second to fourth year Milne Bay students
13.4 If the President or Vice President discontinues from studies, the runner-up for the respective post shall take the office. This shall also be applied to the rest of the executive positions
13.5 In the event that Item 14.4 is not applicable, there can be e re-election called by the President or Vice President and executives to appoint a new executive committee.
13.6 No person shall hold more than one position on the Management Committee at any one time. A person shall cease to be a member of the Management Committee at the conclusion of the Annual General Forum which follows his/her election and he/she will be eligible for re-election.
13.7 A quorum of the Management Committees shall be half of its members plus one.
13.8 If the President or Vice President is unable to attend, then a chairperson nominated by the meeting shall chair that meeting.
13.9 A member of the management committee may lose his or her seat on the committee for either of the following;
• Absence from a total of three meetings or two consecutive meetings without leave of absence.
• Found not to be a financial member.
• Discontinues from Studies
• Suspension from studies by the University Student Disciplinary Counsel (USDC)
13.10 Executive Election Process: Association Executives will be selected by a nomination and election process. Executives will be elected any time before the 2nd semester exams commence and will serve a term of 1 academic year following the university almanac. The time between elections and new term shall serve as a transition period for new executives.

14.0 ASSOCIATION’S PATRON
14.1 A mature and willing Milne Bay who permanently resides in Lae City and who possess quality leadership skills and attributes is eligible to become the association’s patron. The Patron will be nominated by the executives and requested by a formal request letter. Their term will last for a period of one year and can be reappointed annually upon formal request by the Association. The Patrons duties shall include: *Please see Patron’s Agreement*
14.1.1 Meeting with the organization executive officers on a regular basis.
14.1.2 Attending organization meetings and activities.
14.2 The Patron shall have no voting rights in the association.

15.0 POWERS OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE
15.1 The Executive Committee shall carry out the day-to-day running of the Association and shall have the power to:
15.1.1 Administer the finances, appoint bankers, and direct the opening of banking accounts for specific purposes and to transfer funds from one account to another, and to close any such account;
15.1.2 Fix the manner in which such banking accounts shall be operated upon, providing the Management Committee passes all payments;
15.1.3 Fix fees and subscriptions payable by members and decide such levies, fines and charges as is deemed necessary and advisable, and to enforce payment thereof;
15.1.4 Adjudicate on all matters brought before it which in any way affect the Association.
15.1.5 Cause minutes to be made of all proceedings at meetings of the Committee and General Meetings of members;
15.1.6 Make, amend and rescind rulings and By-laws;
15.1.7 Have the power to form and appoint any subcommittee/s as required for specific purposes;
15.1.8 May at their discretion employ a person or persons to carry out certain duties required by the Association, at salaries or remunerations for such period of time, as may be deemed necessary
15.1.9 Should a vacancy occur on the Management Committee during the season, the Management Committee shall appoint a successor until the next Annual General Meeting Refer to Sub-Section 9.5 and 9.6 of act
15.1.10 Appoint an officer/s or agent of the Management Committee to have custody of the Association’s records, documents and securities.

16.0 PENALTIES FOR THE EXECUTIVES
16.1 Penalties for the executives shall be SUSPENSION or TERMINATION if they are found guilty of the following crimes and depending on the severity of the crimes;
16.1.1 Misuse of office and the powers entrusted by the members
16.1.2 Misuse of association funds
16.1.3 Bribery of any category
16.2 The executives responsible shall be cleared to resume their duties upon facing full charges of the crimes committed

17.0 AUDITOR
17.1 The Annual General Forum shall elect or appoint an Auditor or Auditors.
17.2The Auditor/s shall examine and audit all the books and accounts of the Association annually, and have the power to call for all books, papers, accounts, receipts etc.., of the Association and report thereon to the Annual General Forum.

18.0 MEETINGS
18.1 Annual General Forum
18.1.1 The Annual General Forum of the Association shall be held once a year and shall be decided by the Executive Committees if and when necessary.
18.1.2 The Secretary shall give at least two (2) days’ notice of the date of the Annual General Forum, to members.
18.1.3 All financial members may attend the Annual General Forum.
18.1.4 The quorum at the Annual General Forum, shall be a minimum of half plus one member. The meeting shall be adjourned for one week if there is no quorum after 30 minutes of the appointed time in the notice for the opening of the Meeting
18.1.5 The agenda for the Annual General Forum shall be;
• Opening of Forum
• Opening Prayer
• Apologies
• Welcome Speech by President/Patron/Staff Representative to the New Intakes (1st Year Milne Bay Students)
• Presentation/Distribution of the MBSSA constitution to the Members
• Presentation of Status/Progress Report
• Adoption of Status/Progress Report
• Presentation of Treasurer’s statement
• Election of New Executive and appointment of Auditor
• Vote of thanks to outgoing Executive
• Determination of Annual Membership Fee
• Notice/s of Motion
• Urgent general business
• Closure
18.2 General Meetings
18.2.1 General Meetings may be called by the Management Committee or at the request of the President and Secretary or on the written request of not less than three (3) members of the Association.
18.2.2 The Secretary shall give at least two (2) days’ notice, in writing, of the day of the General Meeting to the members. Notice of General Meetings shall set out clearly the business for which the meeting has been called. No other business shall be dealt with at that General Meeting.
18.2.3 The quorum at the General Meeting shall be a minimum of 100 students.

19.0 VOTING
19.1 Voting powers at the Annual General Forum and General Meetings:
19.1.1 The President shall be entitled to a deliberate vote and, in the event of a tied vote, the President shall exercise a casting vote.
19.1.2 Each individual financial member present shall have one (1) vote.
19.2 Voting powers at Management Committee Meetings:
19.2.1 The President shall be entitled to a deliberate vote, and, in the event of a tied vote, the President shall exercise a casting vote.
19.2.2 Each individual committee member present shall have one (1) vote.

20.0 ASSOCIATION FUNDS
20.1 Means of acquiring funds will include collection of membership dues (Section 10 of this Act), implementation of approved fundraising events throughout the year, and money collected from the Milne Bay Provincial Government and sponsors.

21.0 FINANCE
21.1 All funds of the association shall be deposited into the association’s accounts at such bank or recognized financial institution as the Management Committee may determine.
21.2 All accounts due by the association shall be paid by cash or cheque after having being passed for the payment at the Management Committee meeting and when immediate payment is necessary, account/s shall be paid and the action endorsed at the next Management Committee meeting.
21.3 The Secretary shall not spend more than a set amount Petty Cash without the consent of the Management Committee, and shall keep a record of such expenditure in a Petty Cash book.
21.4 A statement showing the financial position of the association shall be tabled at each Management Committee meeting by the Treasurer.
21.5 A statement of income and expenditure, Assets and liabilities shall be submitted to the Annual General meeting. The auditor’s report shall be attached to such financial report.
21.6 The financial year of the association shall commence after the annual general forum (beginning of the academic year) each year. The accounts, book and all financial records of the association shall be audited each year.
21.7 The signatories to the association’s account/s will be the Treasurer and two of the following executives;
1. President
2. Vice President
In the event that both the president and the vice president or treasurer are not available, appointed members of the executives or caretakers of the association are permitted to be the signatories.
21.8 All property and income of the association will solely to the promotion of the objectives of the association and no part of the property or income shall be paid or otherwise distributed, directly, or indirectly, to members, except in good faith in the promotion of these objects.

22.0 COMMON SEAL
(A rubber stamp on which is engraved the association’s name)
The common seal of the association shall be kept in the care of the president. The seal shall be used or affixed to any deed or document except pursuant to a resolution of the Management Committee and in the presence of at least the president and two members of the committee, both of whom shall subscribe their names as witnesses.

23.0 AMMENDMENTS TO THE CONSITUTION AND BY-LAWS
23.1 No alteration, repeal or addition shall be made to the constitution except at the Annual General Meeting, or General Meeting, called for that purpose and notice of all motions to alter, repeal or add to the constitution shall be given to the members seven days prior to the Annual General Forum, or three days prior to the General Meeting called for such purpose.
23.2 The secretary shall forward such notices of motion to each management committee member at least five days prior to a general meeting.
23.3 Alterations to the by-laws can be made only at management committee meetings provided noticed of the proposed alteration/s has been duly notified to committee members.
23.4 Such motions, or any part thereof, shall be of no effect unless passed by a 75% majority (Special Resolution) of those present and entitled to a vote at the annual general forum, general meeting or management committee meeting, as the case may be.
23.5 Within 14 days of the passing of the special resolution, the secretary shall notify the PNG UNITECH Milne Bay Students and Staffs Association of the amendment.

24.0 DISSOLUTION
If, on the winding up of the association, any property of the association remains after satisfaction of the depts and liabilities of the association and the costs, charges and expenses of that winding up, that property shall be distributed
(a) another incorporated association having objects similar to those of the association; or
(b) For charitable or benevolent purposes, which incorporated association or purposes, as the case requires, shall be determined by resolution of the members.

25.0 ENABLING CLAUSE
This constitution shall go into effect upon approval of two-thirds vote in favor by all present voting members. This constitution shall replace and render null all previous constitutions, procedures, practices, and precedents for this organization.

26.0 VACANCY OF OFFICE
a) In case of resignation or removal of any officer, an interim acting officer shall be appointed by the executive board.
b) The president shall call for an election within 14 days after vacancy of any office.
c) Elections shall be conducted as stated in the by-laws.
d) Should the office of president become vacant, the vice-president should complete the president’s unexpired term and call for an election for vice-president consistent with elections process set forth in the by-laws.

27.0 SOURCE OF FUNDS
The funds of the associations may be derived from various sources like the Milne Bay Provincial Government, donations and grants from business houses, individuals, membership subscription, fee and levies, payable by members and such other sources as determined by the executives.

28.0 REGISTERED ADDRESS
The registered address of the Association is;
a) The address determined from time to time by the executives; or
b) If the executives has not determined the address to be the registered address, the postal address of the University.

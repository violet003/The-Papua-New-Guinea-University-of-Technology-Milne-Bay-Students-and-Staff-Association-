PURPOSE:
This Association Profile aims to identify and show the PNGUOT Milne Bay Students and Staff Association's (MBSSA) goals and aims and achieving them through unity and networking.
In adherence to our association's established goals, the association will serve as the bridge between the provincial government and students in terms of assistance following
The mission statement explains and describes the work of our association. It captures the core ideas laid out for the association.

ASSOCIATION HISTORY:

This year alone, our association aspires to do more. Especially in the past few years, our association has been dormant with matters of the association. This included areas of responsibility and lack of proper leadership.To mention two main factors:

(1). Leadership: The previous leaders had a lack of willingness and genuine leaders to oversee the association. Hence that resulted in our association being dormant.
(2). Non-cooperation from members of the association which resulted in a dismantled association.

This year, we sought to address these existing issues and possibly make change for the better and aim to achieve the objectives of our association. Few of our objectives are stated in the mission statement along with values and vision to fulfill for our association. It is important to note that to achieve theses stated objectives, a collaborative effort is demanded by all.

MISSION STATEMENT:

The Papua New Guinea University of Technology Milne Bay Students and Staff Association (MBSSA) is an existing association of this university. Hence, our mission statements are as-stated below:

1. Mission

a) Grow in the areas of academy, socially, eco-socially, etc. in the university and abroad
b) Carry out fundraising activities effectively and smartly
c) Reach out through awareness programs to schools, local communities, churches and other avenues
d) Establish stakeholders and ties for the association (IPA, etc.)

2. Goals
a) Aim to achieve mission
b) Well, equipped for anything, when and where need be.
c) Have a good financial or monetary budget for the association at all times

3. Values

a) Maintain honesty and transparency in all association related matters
b) Uphold the constitution of the association
c) Association executives and leaders to carry out their roles and responsibilities with dignity
d) Maintain conduct of rules and proficiency
e) Follow and lead by example

4. Vision

a) A reputable association in the next two to three years
b) Functional organizational association continually
c) Well, establish in almost all areas anticipated
d) Be an association that others can look upon

MANAGEMENT PROCESS

Under management as a process, management is considered as a continuing activity made up of basic management functions. The process is ongoing and continuing. It assumes a cyclical character. The leaders of our association shall follow the following process for managing the association.
1. PLANNING
Denotes the determination of short-to-long-range plans to achieve the objectives of organization. According to Henry Fayol - "purveyance, which is an essential element of planning, covers not merely looking into the future but making provisions for it. A plan is then a projected course of actionâ.
2. ORGANISING
Organizing includes putting life into the plan by bringing together personnel, capital, machinery, materials etc., to execute the plans. While, planning decides what management wants to do, organizing provides an effective machine for achieving the plans.
3. DIRECTION
Means stimulating and motivation of personnel of the organization according to predetermined plans.
4. MANAGEMENT
Ensuring executives and members are carrying out their tasks to achieve specific goals of the association and that instructions regarding the plans set in place are being followed. Also controlling the resources being used.
